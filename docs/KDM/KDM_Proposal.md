## Executive Summary

Strategic Value Plus Solutions is pleased to submit this proposal to design, build, and operate a comprehensive digital platform for the KDM & Associates Consortium. Our approach leverages our existing Strategic Value+ platform infrastructure, enabling rapid deployment at significantly reduced cost while delivering enterprise-grade functionality tailored to your consortium's unique needs.

The KDM Consortium platform will serve as the digital foundation for building a vetted ecosystem of small and medium-sized companies that can win more government and commercial contracts. By combining opportunity identification, intelligent team assembly, proposal orchestration, and strategic buyer access pathways, the platform will create measurable value for consortium members while generating sustainable revenue for both KDM and Strategic Value Plus.

## Understanding Your Mission

KDM & Associates has established a clear and compelling vision: to build a vetted ecosystem where small and medium-sized businesses with proven capabilities can consistently win government and commercial contracts. Your target members are companies with ten to two hundred employees operating in priority verticals including logistics, cybersecurity, information technology, training, engineering, energy resilience, and manufacturing services. These organizations possess relevant capabilities and past performance but often lack consistent access to opportunities, teaming partners, and proposal development resources.

The consortium addresses this challenge by providing four core value drivers. First, opportunity identification ensures members receive curated intelligence on relevant procurement opportunities. Second, best-fit team assembly leverages objective criteria to match members with complementary capabilities. Third, proposal orchestration provides a repeatable workflow from capture through submission. Fourth, buyer access pathways create credible connections to government agencies, prime contractors, and commercial buyers through monthly briefings, quarterly showcases, and strategic introductions.

Your revenue model centers on a Core Capture Membership priced at $1,750 per month, supplemented by pursuit-specific add-on packages for complex proposals, event ticketing revenue, sponsorship opportunities, and training courses. The business model includes a fifty-fifty revenue split between KDM and Strategic Value Plus on net program revenue, creating aligned incentives for platform success and member satisfaction.

## What We Will Build

While our existing platform provides the foundation, we will develop specific features tailored to the KDM Consortium's unique requirements. The payment infrastructure will integrate Stripe Connect to enable membership billing, event ticketing, and automatic revenue splitting between KDM and Strategic Value Plus. This system will handle the fifty-fifty revenue share transparently with automated monthly settlement reporting.

We will implement a comprehensive membership tier system that manages enrollment, onboarding, tier-based access controls, and renewal workflows. The event ticketing system will support multiple ticket types, promotional codes, payment processing, automated confirmations, and mobile-friendly check-in capabilities. Our sponsor management module will track sponsor tiers, benefits, fulfillment status, and provide sponsors with portal access to monitor their investment.

The pursuit brief workflow will enable weekly opportunity triage, member-only intelligence distribution, and automated matching between opportunities and qualified members. Email notification systems will keep members informed of new opportunities, event reminders, proposal deadlines, and important consortium updates. Public-facing event listing pages will promote consortium activities and drive registration.

Our landing page builder will enable rapid creation of targeted pages for cohort candidates, conference attendees, and webinar participants. The team assembly algorithm will recommend optimal team configurations based on capability relevance, capacity availability, compliance requirements, geographic location, and buyer proximity. Administrative reporting dashboards will provide real-time visibility into member metrics, pipeline activity, buyer engagement, and financial performance.

## Implementation Timeline

Our implementation follows a phased approach designed to deliver value quickly while building toward comprehensive functionality. Phase One focuses on launching the minimum viable product within six to eight weeks. During weeks one and two, we establish the payment foundation by integrating Stripe Connect, implementing the membership tier system, building payment workflows for dues and tickets, and setting up email notification services.

Weeks three and four concentrate on the event system, including public event listing pages, event registration workflows, ticketing with payment processing, automated confirmations, and QR code generation for mobile check-in. During weeks five and six, we customize the member portal with KDM branding, implement member directory enhancements with tier badges, build the pursuit brief workflow, and organize the resource library with appropriate categorization.

Weeks seven and eight focus on administrative capabilities and launch preparation. We build the sponsor management interface, create administrative reporting dashboards, automate settlement statement generation, conduct comprehensive testing, fix identified issues, and prepare for production launch. At the conclusion of Phase One, you will have a fully functional platform with five founding members onboarded, your first ticketed event completed, and monthly reporting systems operational.

Phase Two, spanning weeks nine through sixteen, adds growth-oriented features including the team assembly algorithm, proposal collaboration workspace, buyer relationship management system, advanced analytics dashboard, landing page builder, email automation sequences, and Zoom integration for virtual events. This phase targets growth from ten to fifteen active members with one to two pursuits launched monthly and your first buyer briefing successfully hosted.

Phase Three, covering weeks seventeen through twenty-four, focuses on scaling to thirty members and optimizing operations. We add content management capabilities for blog posts and articles, video hosting and archiving, CMMC education pathways, enhanced mobile responsiveness, learning management system integration if needed, and advanced reporting with data export capabilities. This phase delivers monthly buyer briefings, quarterly showcase events, three to six active pursuits per month, and established sponsor revenue streams.

## Investment and Return

The investment required for Phase One, our minimum viable product, ranges from $43,000 to $70,500. This includes full-stack development, user interface and experience design, project management and coordination, and quality assurance testing. Phase Two requires an additional investment of $25,000 to $35,000 for growth features. Phase Three requires $20,000 to $30,000 for scaling and optimization capabilities. The total six-month investment ranges from $88,000 to $135,500.

Monthly operational costs are modest and predictable. Stripe charges standard processing fees of 2.9% plus thirty cents per transaction. Email services cost fifteen to fifty dollars monthly based on volume. Database and storage services range from twenty-five to one hundred dollars monthly depending on usage. Hosting services cost twenty dollars monthly. Domain and SSL certificates cost fifteen dollars monthly. Document signing services range from twenty-nine to ninety-nine dollars monthly. Total monthly operational costs before transaction fees range from one hundred fifty to three hundred dollars.

The platform run-cost allowance, as specified in our Letter of Intent, is set at fifty dollars per active paid member per month. This allowance covers hosting, monitoring, support, administrative operations, and tooling overhead tied to member services. This cost structure will be reassessed quarterly based on actual usage patterns to ensure fairness and transparency.

The return on this investment is compelling. With thirty members paying $1,750 monthly, the consortium generates $52,500 in monthly recurring revenue, translating to $315,000 in annual run rate. Under the fifty-fifty revenue share agreement, both KDM and Strategic Value Plus receive $157,500 annually. Additional revenue from pursuit packs, event tickets, sponsorships, and training courses provides upside beyond the base membership model.

## Success Metrics

We propose measuring platform success across four key dimensions: platform health, member engagement, financial performance, and business outcomes. Platform health metrics include maintaining 99.9% uptime, ensuring page load times under two seconds, achieving one hundred percent mobile responsiveness across all pages, and maintaining zero security incidents. These technical metrics ensure members have a reliable, fast, and secure experience.

Member engagement metrics track onboarding completion rates above eighty percent, monthly retention rates above ninety percent, active participation rates above sixty percent, and profile completion rates above seventy-five percent. These metrics indicate whether members find value in the platform and remain engaged over time. High engagement correlates directly with member satisfaction and renewal rates.

Financial metrics focus on monthly recurring revenue growth of twenty percent month-over-month during the first six months, pursuit pack revenue reaching $5,000 to $10,000 monthly by month six, event ticket revenue of $10,000 to $20,000 quarterly, and sponsor revenue of $15,000 to $30,000 quarterly. These metrics demonstrate the platform's ability to generate diversified revenue streams beyond base membership fees.

Business outcome metrics measure the consortium's impact on member success. We track pursuits launched, targeting one to two monthly during months one through three and three to six monthly during months four through six. We monitor buyer briefing frequency with a minimum of one per month. We measure member login frequency, targeting an average of three or more times weekly. We track resource downloads, targeting five or more per member monthly. We monitor proposal submission timeliness with an eighty percent on-time rate target. These metrics demonstrate whether the platform enables members to pursue and win more business.
