## Executive Summary

This project brief outlines the requirements, opportunities, and strategic pathway for Red Arts Capital portfolio companies to become qualified suppliers for Toyota Battery Manufacturing North Carolina (TBMNC). Red Arts Capital, a Chicago-based private equity firm with a $400 million fund focused on supply chain and logistics businesses, has assembled a powerful portfolio of companies with complementary capabilities that align exceptionally well with Toyota's EV battery manufacturing supply chain needs.

### Key Opportunity
Toyota Battery Manufacturing North Carolina represents a **multi-billion dollar opportunity** for qualified suppliers. With three battery types in production (hybrid, plug-in hybrid, and full EV) and distribution expanding beyond Kentucky in 2026, the timing is optimal for Red Arts portfolio companies to establish themselves as Tier-1 suppliers.

### Strategic Advantage
Red Arts Capital's MBE certification, combined with the diverse capabilities across its portfolio, positions the group uniquely to capture significant market share in Toyota's supply chain while meeting diversity supplier requirements.

### Detailed Certification Descriptions

#### ISO 9001:2015 - Quality Management System
**Who needs it:** ALL suppliers to Toyota  
**What it covers:**
- Customer focus and satisfaction
- Leadership and organizational context
- Risk-based thinking and planning
- Support resources and competence
- Operational planning and control
- Performance evaluation
- Continuous improvement

**Why Toyota requires it:** Ensures suppliers have documented, repeatable processes that deliver consistent quality.

#### IATF 16949:2016 - Automotive Quality Management System
**Who needs it:** Manufacturing suppliers providing parts, components, or assemblies  
**What it covers:**
- All ISO 9001 requirements PLUS:
- Product safety requirements
- Automotive-specific process controls
- PPAP (Production Part Approval Process)
- APQP (Advanced Product Quality Planning)
- FMEA (Failure Mode and Effects Analysis)
- MSA (Measurement System Analysis)
- SPC (Statistical Process Control)

**Why Toyota requires it:** Automotive industry standard ensuring suppliers can meet the rigorous quality demands of vehicle manufacturing.

#### ISO 14001:2015 - Environmental Management System
**Who needs it:** Recommended for all suppliers; may be contractually required  
**What it covers:**
- Environmental policy and objectives
- Legal compliance
- Resource efficiency
- Waste reduction
- Pollution prevention
- Continuous environmental improvement

**Why Toyota values it:** Aligns with Toyota's sustainability goals and ESG requirements.

#### ISO 45001:2018 - Occupational Health & Safety
**Who needs it:** Manufacturing and logistics operations, especially those handling hazardous materials  
**What it covers:**
- Workplace safety management
- Hazard identification and risk assessment
- Worker participation
- Emergency preparedness
- Incident investigation
- Performance monitoring

**Why it matters for TBMNC:** Battery manufacturing and logistics involve hazardous materials requiring robust safety protocols.
